# Traceability and Recall SOP

## Purpose
Guarantee one-up one-down traceability for every batch so that product
can be identified, contained and recalled quickly if a safety or legal
issue is discovered.

## Scope
All raw material intake, production runs, finished goods and despatches
covered by the compliance dashboard.

## References
- BRCGS Food Safety Issue 9, clause 3.9 (traceability) and 3.11 (recall)
- UK General Food Regulations 2004

## Linkage Rules
Every production record must carry:
- A short batch code in the format single letter followed by four digits
  and a checksum letter, for example F0001A.
- The raw material batch or batches consumed to produce it.
- The supplier lot reference for each raw material.
- The harvest, defrost or intake date as applicable.
- The customer order reference once despatched.

## Verification
A mock recall is performed at least twice per calendar year and must
reconstruct the full forward and backward chain for any randomly
selected batch within four hours, including quantities produced and
quantities still in stock or at customer sites.

## Compliance Target
The traceability service level objective is at least 90 percent of
production records with a non-empty raw material linkage at all times.
Any gap below 90 percent triggers a corrective action request.

## Escalation
If a safety risk is confirmed, the Technical Manager initiates the
recall procedure, contacts affected customers within two hours, and
notifies the local Environmental Health Officer the same working day.
