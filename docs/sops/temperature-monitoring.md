# Temperature Monitoring SOP

## Purpose
Define how chilled and frozen storage areas are monitored so that finished
product remains within safe limits at every stage of the supply chain.

## Scope
Applies to every chilled zone, freezer, blast chiller, coldstore and
despatch bay operated by the facility. Excludes ambient dry goods
storage, which is covered by a separate SOP.

## References
- BRCGS Food Safety Issue 9, clauses 4.14, 6.1 and 8.5.1
- Codex Alimentarius General Principles of Food Hygiene
- EU Regulation 852/2004

## Limits and Targets
| Area type      | Operating limit       | Alert threshold      |
|----------------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| Chilled        | 0 degC to +4 degC     | +4 degC for >15 min  |
| Coldstore      | -2 degC to +2 degC    | +2 degC for >10 min  |
| Freezer        | at or below -18 degC  | -16 degC for >30 min |
| Despatch bay   | 0 degC to +6 degC     | +6 degC for >20 min  |

## Monitoring
Each area has a calibrated probe logging at least one reading every
fifteen minutes. Readings are written to the compliance database and
flagged as an excursion when the alert threshold is breached. A
verification check by the shift lead is recorded on each handover.

## Excursion Handling
When an excursion alert is raised:
1. Quarantine affected stock pending QA review.
2. Investigate root cause (door left open, door seal failure,
   refrigeration fault, power loss).
3. Record corrective action and decide whether product is safe for
   release, requires a concession, or must be disposed of.
4. Retain records for a minimum of twelve months.

## Compliance Target
The service level objective is at least 95 percent of logged readings
within the operating limit over any rolling thirty day window.
